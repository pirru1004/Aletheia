Two signals, four outcomes:
  • Lit flare + low methane → operational, combusting efficiently.
  • Lit flare + high methane → operational, poor combustion.
  • No flare + high methane → venting, leak, or unlit flare. ← current
  • No flare + no methane → site appears inactive.

Near-normal NO₂ with elevated CO points to incomplete, low-temperature combustion — a possible upset condition, consistent with the venting signature.
Cross-indicator reconciliation rules:
  • NO₂↑ + CO↑ → active combustion, broad activity confirmed.
  • NO₂↑ + CO normal → lean (high-temp) combustion, well-tuned refinery.
  • NO₂ normal + CO↑ → incomplete / low-temp combustion, possible upset. ← current
  • SAR slick + no incident report → candidate undisclosed release.
  • All atmospheric normal + reported high activity → candidate over-reporting.
SAR is not exercised here (onshore shale gas, no water).
